# AV2 Arriving: What We Know, and What We Don't Know

Sep 19, 2025

AOMedia announced AV2 set for release at the end of 2025. Improvements over AV1 include:
- Significantly better compression performance
- Enhanced support for AR/VR content
- Split-screen and multi-view delivery
- Improved handling of screen content
- Operation across a broader visual quality range

Like AV1, AV2 will be offered under AOMedia's royalty-free patent policy.

**Adoption Survey:**
- 53% of AOM members plan to adopt AV2 within 12 months of release
- 88% expect implementation within two years

## Key Unknowns

As of September 2025, there are no published real-world encoding or decoding benchmarks, no playback performance measurements, and no field reports from device makers.

## IP Question

AOMedia stresses royalty-free patent policy, but third-party patent pools (Access Advance, Avanci, Sisvel) may claim royalties on AV2 encoded content/decoders, similar to AV1.

## Deployment Timeline Estimates

**Desktop:** Early software playback in 2026, led by YouTube/Facebook. Premium adoption waits on DRM (Widevine, PlayReady, FairPlay).

**Mobile:** Real deployment depends on hardware support from Qualcomm, MediaTek, Apple. Vendors have invested heavily in VVC, making fast AV2 integration unlikely. Apple waited until late 2023 to enable AV1 hardware decode (5 years after AV1 launch). Similar delay for AV2 would push efficient mobile playback into early 2030s.

**Smart TVs and Dongles:** Could move faster than mobile if Netflix/YouTube push. But meaningful market penetration unlikely before 2027-28.

## Conclusion

AV2 is coming with promised compression gains, but timing of relevance depends on real-world playback support. For general-purpose content, AV2's value proposition looks uncomfortably close to AV1. It could differentiate in AR/VR, split-screen, and multi-view delivery.
