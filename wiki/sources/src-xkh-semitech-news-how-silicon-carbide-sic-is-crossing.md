# How Silicon Carbide (SiC) is Crossing into AR Glasses

## 来源

- **原始文件**: raw/industry/smart-glasses/xkh-semitech-news-how-silicon-carbide-sic-is-crossing.md
- **提取日期**: 2026-05-02

## 摘要

Silicon carbide (SiC) is emerging as a key material for AR glasses waveguides due to its high refractive index (>2.6), excellent thermal conductivity, and high hardness. Meta's Orion AR glasses demonstrated a 70-degree FoV using SiC waveguides, while XINKEHUI and MOD MICRO-NANO are partnering to develop commercial SiC diffraction waveguide lenses.

## 关键要点

- SiC has a refractive index over 2.6, far exceeding traditional glass (1.5-1.9) and resin materials
- Meta Orion achieved 70° FoV using SiC-etched waveguides combined with microLEDs
- XINKEHUI developed the world's first 12-inch high-purity semi-insulating SiC single crystal substrate in December 2024
- MOD MICRO-NANO's second-gen SiC AR glasses lenses weigh only 2.7g with 0.55mm thickness
- Current challenge: a single SiC lens for Meta Orion costs approximately $1,000

## 产品/技术细节

- **Material properties**: High refractive index (>2.6), thermal conductivity hundreds of times greater than glass, hardness second only to diamond
- **Meta Orion**: 70° FoV, silicon carbide-etched waveguides with microLEDs, slanted etching technology
- **MOD MICRO-NANO 2nd gen**: 2.7g weight, 0.55mm thickness, lighter than regular sunglasses
- **Processing**: Slanted etching is a non-traditional grating technology; no prior equipment existed for etching SiC waveguides before 2019
- **Cost barrier**: A single SiC lens costs ~$1,000; 12-inch wafers can produce 8-9 pairs of AR glasses lenses
